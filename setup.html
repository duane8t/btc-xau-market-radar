Setup & Trading Guide

How to install MetaTrader 5 and trade using BTC XAU Market Radar

What This Service Is

BTC XAU Market Radar is a market awareness tool. It alerts you when Bitcoin (BTC) or Gold (XAU) become active due to liquidity, volatility, or session-driven movement.

This is not a signal service.
You decide if, when, and how to trade.

What You Need Before Starting

  • A Windows PC or laptop
  • A trading account (demo or live)
  • Telegram installed and logged in

Beginners are strongly encouraged to start on a demo account.

Step 1 – Install MetaTrader 5

  • Download MetaTrader 5 from your broker or the official MetaTrader website
  • Install and open the platform
  • Log in using the account details provided by your broker

MetaTrader 5 is the charting and execution platform used for trading.

Step 2 – Enable BTCUSD & XAUUSD

  • Open Market Watch (Ctrl + M)
  • Right-click → Show All (if symbols are hidden)
  • Locate BTCUSD and XAUUSD
  • Drag each symbol onto the chart area

Step 3 – Set Up Your Charts

  • Set timeframe to M15 (15-minute)
  • Use candlestick view
  • Keep charts clean (no indicators required)
Clean charts help you read structure and price reaction more clearly.

Step 4 – Join Telegram & Read Alerts

  • Join the BTC XAU Market Radar Telegram channel
  • Enable notifications
  • Pin the channel for quick access

An alert means:
“Pay attention — conditions have changed.”

How to Trade Using the Alerts

  • Open the relevant chart (BTC or XAU)
  • Mark recent highs, lows, and structure
  • Wait for confirmation (rejection, continuation, break & retest)
  • Plan entry, stop loss, and take profit
Do not chase price.
If the move has already happened, wait or skip the trade.

When NOT to Trade

  • No alert = no trade
  • Choppy or range-bound price
  • Late entries after large impulsive moves

Silence from the radar is intentional and protective.

Questions & Answers

Is this a signal service?

No. This service does not provide buy or sell signals.

Does this place trades automatically?

No. All trades are manual.

Why are there sometimes no alerts?

The radar filters low-quality market conditions. No alert usually means it is safer to stay out.

How often will alerts appear?

There is no fixed number. Some days may have none.

Bitcoin moved a lot but I got no alert — why?

Large moves do not always involve tradeable liquidity or structure.

Does this work 24/7?

BTC runs 24/7. XAU is most active during London and New York sessions.

Can beginners use this?

Yes — but start on demo and trade small.

Is profitability guaranteed?

No. Trading involves risk and outcomes vary.

Is this financial advice?

No. This service provides market awareness only.
